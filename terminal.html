'Desperate' to Travel, but Rising Costs Point to 'Uneven' Summer

[ Article Hero Image — 3:2 ]
Skift Take

The travel industry's K-shaped recovery has hardened into something structural. High-income households are spending without restraint; budget travelers are trading down or staying home. If your summer revenue plan assumes broad market growth, this won't be your summer.

Memorial Day weekend is shaping up to be one of the busiest travel periods on record, but beneath the headline numbers lies a story the industry isn't telling yet: this summer won't look like any summer before it.

High-income households are booking international trips, upgrading to premium cabins, and extending stays. Meanwhile, middle and lower-income travelers are shortening trips, choosing drive-to destinations, and waiting for last-minute deals that may not come.

× Intelligence: Drive-to Markets

Drive-to destinations (within 200 miles) now represent 58% of Memorial Day bookings vs. 43% in 2019. This isn't just cost-driven — it's a permanent shift in how budget travelers think about vacation planning.

Research 2026 Consumer Spending Report

The data is stark. Households earning over $150,000 are spending 18% more on travel than they did in 2019. Households under $75,000 are spending 12% less. And the gap is widening, not closing.

× Intelligence: Spending Gap Analysis

This isn't a temporary dip. Income-stratified analysis shows the bifurcation accelerating quarter-over-quarter since Q3 2024. Your revenue models need to account for two completely different demand curves.

Event Skift Global Forum: "The Two-Speed Market" keynote

For hotel revenue managers, this isn't an abstract problem. It's a pricing challenge that plays out property by property: which segments do you optimize for, and which do you let walk?

Airlines face a similar calculus. Premium cabin load factors are at all-time highs. Economy is soft. The industry's response has been to add more premium seats and shrink coach. That works until it doesn't.

The risk is that the industry is pricing itself into a corner. If budget travelers permanently exit the market, the entire volume model breaks. And if a recession hits the high end, there's no safety net below.

× Intelligence: Strategic Risk

Three scenarios to model: (1) Budget segment stays depressed through 2027, (2) Premium cools Q4 2026, (3) Both segments soften simultaneously. Only one of these has historical precedent.
